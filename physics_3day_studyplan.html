Conservation of Linear Momentum from Newton's Laws
Show that if net external force = 0, total momentum is constant. Use F = dp/dt → dp/dt = 0 → p = constant. Derive from Newton's 3rd law (action-reaction). Also prove objects of same mass exchange velocities in elastic collision.

Centripetal Force & Acceleration Derivation
Derive a = v²/r for uniform circular motion using change in velocity vector. Show centripetal force F = mv²/r. Draw direction of acceleration toward centre. Also: Show centripetal force does no work (F⊥v).

03
Escape Velocity Derivation (v = √2gR)
Use energy conservation: KE + PE = 0 at infinity → ½mv² = GMm/R → v = √(2GM/R) = √(2gR). Also show: escape velocity = √2 × orbital velocity. Dimensional check is a bonus MCQ topic.

04
Drift Velocity → Current Relation (I = nAev_d)
Derive: In time t, electrons travel v_d·t. Number of electrons in volume A·v_d·t = n·A·v_d·t. Charge = n·A·v_d·t·e. Current I = nAev_d. Hence J = nev_d. Also derive J = σE and connect to Ohm's law.

Projectile Motion — Path, T, H_max, Range
Show path is parabolic (y = x tanθ − gx²/2u²cos²θ). Derive: T = 2u sinθ/g, H = u²sin²θ/2g, R = u²sin2θ/g. Prove maximum range at θ=45°. Show same range for θ and (90°−θ).

Banking of a Curved Road — Banking Angle
Resolve forces on vehicle on banked road: N cosθ = mg, N sinθ = mv²/r. Divide → tanθ = v²/rg. With friction: tan(θ+φ) = v²/rg. For optimum speed, derive v = √(rg tanθ). Explain why roads are banked.

07
Orbital Velocity & Time Period of Satellite
At height h: gravitational force = centripetal force → GMm/(R+h)² = mv²/(R+h) → v = √(GM/(R+h)) = R√(g/(R+h)). Time period T = 2π(R+h)/v. Height of satellite: h = (T²R²g/4π²)^(1/3) − R.

09
Conical Pendulum — Time Period & Frequency
T cosθ = mg, T sinθ = mω²r. Divide → tanθ = ω²r/g. Since r = l sinθ: ω² = g/l cosθ → T = 2π√(l cosθ/g). Difference from simple pendulum: bob moves in horizontal circle, not vertical plane. f = (1/2π)√(g/l cosθ).

10
Mirror Formula Derivation (1/f = 1/v + 1/u)
Using geometry of concave mirror: △ABC and △A'B'C are similar → A'B'/AB = CB'/CB. Also △ABF and △DEF similar → A'B'/DE = B'F/EF. Combining and using sign convention derive 1/f = 1/v + 1/u. Show m = −v/u.

11
Work-Energy Theorem (W = ΔKE)
From F = ma and v² = u² + 2as → Fs = ½mv² − ½mu². So W_net = ΔKE. Also prove using calculus: W = ∫F·dx = ∫ma·dx = ∫m(dv/dt)·dx = ∫mv·dv = ½mv² − ½mu².

Angle of Repose = Angle of Friction (θ = φ)
On inclined plane at angle θ: N = mg cosθ, f = mg sinθ. At limiting equilibrium: f = μN → mg sinθ = μ mg cosθ → tanθ = μ = tanφ → θ = φ. Derive relation between angle of repose and angle of friction.

Pressure Exerted by Ideal Gas (KTG)
Derive P = (1/3)ρc² using kinetic molecular model. Consider N molecules in cube of side l. Force per collision = 2mv_x/t. Pressure on one wall, then average over all directions → P = Nm⟨v²⟩/3V = (1/3)ρ⟨c²⟩. Hence KE = (3/2)kT.

Series & Parallel Combination of Resistances
Series: same current I, V = V₁+V₂+V₃ → IR = IR₁+IR₂+IR₃ → R = R₁+R₂+R₃. Parallel: same V, I = I₁+I₂+I₃ → V/R = V/R₁+V/R₂+V/R₃ → 1/R = 1/R₁+1/R₂+1/R₃. Explain which increases/decreases resistance.

Newton's Law of Cooling Derivation & Application
Rate of heat loss dQ/dt ∝ (T − T₀). So −ms(dT/dt) = k(T − T₀). Integrate → ln(T − T₀) = −kt/ms + C → T = T₀ + Ae^(−kt/ms). Use to find specific heat capacity of unknown liquid by comparing cooling curves in calorimeter experiment.

📌 Strategy Tips from HISSAN Pattern:

• MCQs: Mechanics dominates — 5 out of 11 MCQs come from it. Know dimensions, circular motion concepts, and optics MCQs cold.
• SAQs (5 marks each): Always prepare Circular Motion, Dynamics, DC Circuits, and Heat SAQs — they appear every single year.
• LAQs (8 marks each): Only 3 LAQs — always one from Mechanics (Newton/Circular/Gravitation), one from Thermodynamics or Optics, one from Modern Physics or Electricity. Practise the derivations fully.
• Numericals: Don't skip — every LAQ and many SAQs include a numerical part. Circular motion, Gravitation, Capacitor, and Nuclear numericals are most repeated.
• Skip entirely (for 3 days): Convection (only MCQ-level), GPS working principle (1-mark descriptive), Recent Trends in Physics (conceptual 1-mark MCQ max — just read once).
